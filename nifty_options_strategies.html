Nifty Options Intraday Trading Strategies

1. Gap-Up Strategy

Scenario: The market opens with a significant gap up.

Price Action: Look for a retracement towards the previous day's close or a key support level.

Entry: Enter a long position when the price shows signs of reversal at the support level.

Stop Loss: Place the stop loss below the support level.

Target: Set a target based on a multiple of the risk or at a key resistance level.

2. Gap-Down Strategy

Scenario: The market opens with a significant gap down.

Price Action: Look for a retracement towards the previous day's close or a key resistance level.

Entry: Enter a short position when the price shows signs of reversal at the resistance level.

Stop Loss: Place the stop loss above the resistance level.

Target: Set a target based on a multiple of the risk or at a key support level.

3. Retail Trader Trap (False Breakout) Strategy

Scenario: The price breaks above a resistance level or below a support level, but the breakout fails.

Price Action: Identify key support and resistance levels. Wait for a breakout and then look for signs of failure.

Entry: Enter a short position if the price breaks above resistance and then reverses, or enter a long position if the price breaks below support and then reverses.

Stop Loss: Place the stop loss above the high of the false breakout (for short positions) or below the low of the false breakout (for long positions).

Target: Set a target based on a multiple of the risk or at the next key support/resistance level.

4. Stop Loss Hunting Strategy

Scenario: Market makers intentionally push the price to trigger stop-loss orders before reversing the price.

Price Action: Identify areas where stop-loss orders are likely to be clustered.

Entry: After the price breaks through the stop-loss cluster and shows signs of reversal, enter a position in the opposite direction.

Stop Loss: Place the stop loss beyond the extreme of the stop-loss hunting move.

Target: Set a target based on a multiple of the risk or at the next key support/resistance level.

5. Support and Resistance Strategy

Scenario: The price approaches a key support or resistance level.

Price Action: Observe how the price reacts at the support or resistance level. Look for signs of reversal or continuation.

Entry: Enter a long position if the price bounces off support with bullish signals, or enter a short position if the price bounces off resistance with bearish signals.

Stop Loss: Place the stop loss below the support level (for long positions) or above the resistance level (for short positions).

Target: Set a target based on a multiple of the risk or at the next key support/resistance level.

6. Trend Following Strategy

Scenario: The market is in a clear uptrend or downtrend.

Price Action: Identify the trend direction using moving averages, trendlines, or other trend indicators.

Entry: Enter a long position on a pullback to a support level or a moving average in an uptrend, or enter a short position on a bounce to a resistance level or a moving average in a downtrend.

Stop Loss: Place the stop loss below the support level or moving average (for long positions) or above the resistance level or moving average (for short positions).

Target: Set a target based on a multiple of the risk or at the next key support/resistance level.

7. Fake Breakout Strategy

Scenario: The price appears to break out of a trading range but quickly reverses.

Price Action: Identify trading ranges and wait for a breakout. Look for signs that the breakout is failing.

Entry: Enter a short position if the price breaks above the range and then reverses back into the range, or enter a long position if the price breaks below the range and then reverses back into the range.

Stop Loss: Place the stop loss above the high of the fake breakout (for short positions) or below the low of the fake breakout (for long positions).

Target: Set a target based on a multiple of the risk or at the opposite end of the trading range.
