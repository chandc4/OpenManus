Itinerary

Day 1: Tokyo - Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

Address: 11 Naitomachi, Shinjuku City, Tokyo 160-0014, Japan

Description: A beautiful garden with diverse landscapes.

Day 2: Tokyo - Asakusa & Meiji Jingu Shrine

Senso-ji Temple Address: 2 Chome-3-1 Asakusa, Taito City, Tokyo 111-0032, Japan

Description: Tokyo's oldest temple.

Meiji Jingu Shrine Address: 1-1 Yoyogikamizonocho, Shibuya City, Tokyo 151-8557, Japan

Description: A peaceful shrine dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken.

Day 3: Hakone

Hakone Open-Air Museum Address: 1121 Ninotaira, Hakone, Ashigarashimo District, Kanagawa 250-0407, Japan

Description: Contemporary sculptures set against the backdrop of mountains.

Day 4: Kyoto - Gion

Description: Explore Gion, Kyoto's geisha district.

Day 5: Kyoto - Fushimi Inari & Kiyomizu-dera

Fushimi Inari Shrine Address: 68 Fukakusa Yabunouchicho, Fushimi Ward, Kyoto, 612-0882, Japan

Description: Famous for its thousands of red torii gates.

Kiyomizu-dera Temple Address: 294 Kiyomizu 1-chome, Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto, 605-0862, Japan

Description: Known for its wooden stage and panoramic views.

Day 6: Nara - Nara Park

Description: Interact with the friendly deer in Nara Park.

Todai-ji Temple Address: 406-1 Zoshicho, Nara, 630-8211, Japan

Description: Houses a giant bronze Buddha statue.

Kasuga Taisha Shrine Address: 160 Kasuganocho, Nara, 630-8212, Japan

Description: Famous for its thousands of lanterns.

Day 7: Kyoto - Tea Ceremony & Zen Meditation

Description: Participate in a traditional tea ceremony and Zen meditation session.

Japanese Phrases

  • Hello: Konnichiwa (こんにちは)
  • Thank you: Arigato (ありがとう)
  • Excuse me: Sumimasen (すみません)
  • Yes: Hai (はい)
  • No: Iie (いいえ)
  • I don't understand: Wakarimasen (わかりません)
  • Where is the restroom?: Toire wa doko desu ka? (トイレはどこですか？)
  • How much is this?: Kore wa ikura desu ka? (これはいくらですか？)

Travel Tips

  • Etiquette: Avoid talking loudly on trains. Don't wear shoes inside homes or temples.
  • Transportation: Purchase a Suica or Pasmo card for easy travel on trains and buses.
  • Money: Have some cash on hand, as some smaller establishments may not accept credit cards.
